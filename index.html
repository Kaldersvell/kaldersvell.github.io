TAKE THE "PAIN" OUT OF A GUI-FREE OS

Kaldersvell allows you to remotely access your RasPi with a planned clean interface that allows you to harness the full power of your Pi for development without learning a whole new set of commands to do so. With Kaldersvell, forget all of the hassle it takes to set up your RasPi and just code.



Accessibility guaranteeed

Anyone with a RasPi and a powersource for it can use Kaldersvell; the necessity for expensive HDMI monitors or pesky adapters is a thing of the past; just by entering the IP address of the Raspberry Pi, you can use Kaldersvell, with no pain to your mind or your wallet.



ELEGANTLY DESIGNED, INTELLIGENTLY CRAFTED

Kaldersvell's engine runs more efficiently than other OS's due to the desire to tinker held by the team; it's streamlined, efficient, and the tools that we built are there for you to make your life easier and your coding more fun. Combine that with our show-stopping UI, there's nothing better to fulfill your IoT dreams.










Kaldersvell is an award-winning operating system for the raspberry pi which allows you to use a friendly user interface to control and develop for internet of things as easily as using your favorite operating systems.









History

  • May 2015

    The Kaldersvell Project was founded by its first and current leader Noah Paladino and his associate Ali Mohamad at the Middlesex County Academy for Science, Mathematics and Engineering Technologies in Edison, New Jersey, with hopes of it being a user friendly Ubuntu-based OS for beginners

  • July 2015 - January 2016

    Noah did bug triaging and confirmation for Ubuntu Gnome Version 15.01, and decides he wants to pivot; Ubuntu Gnome is already doing a great job with a fantastic community - why take away from it and do your own thing? The team decides to refocus.

  • April 2016

    Noah and Ali went to HackBCA III, the largest high school hackathon in the US, and built KaldersvellEDU, a modification to Raspbian which allows for the user to use their RasPi without having to ssh or connect to the Pi via HDMI first - a server would be started by the RasPi which would serve a web-based OS for the user to code on. This was version 0.0.1 of Kaldersvell, and it won Best Developer Tool and rank in the Top 5 Hacks, with a demo on stage.

  • May 2016

    Version 0.1.0 of Kaldersvell is launched. Click here to download it!

  • Future?

    Kaldersvell is just getting started - Version 0.5.0 is slated to launch in the near future, and who knows what'll happen after that?
